Objective

The primary objective of this project is to analyze and explore the sales data to identify key patterns, trends, and factors influencing sales performance. Additionally, we aim to assess the company's shipping efficiency by analyzing shipping time ranges and shipping costs per unit.

Dataset Description

The dataset consists of multiple columns, including 'Order ID', 'Order Date', 'Ship Date', 'Ship Mode', 'Customer ID', 'Customer Name', 'Segment', 'Country', 'City', 'Postal Code', 'Region', 'Product ID', 'Category', 'Sub-Category', 'Product Name', 'Sales', 'Quantity', 'Discount', 'Discount value', 'Profit', 'COGS', 'Shipping time range', 'Year sales', and 'Shipping cost per unit'. Each row represents a unique sales transaction made by the customers of the e-commerce company.

Dataset 

-<a href="https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/monikachdhankhar/excel-dashboard">dataset</a>
 
Analysis and Findings 

Sales show an overall increasing trend from 2014 to 2017, with noticeable peaks in later years. A seasonality can be seen 
in the sales over years. This indicates steady business growth. 

Sales by Category 

Technology is the highest-performing category in terms of sales, followed by Furniture and Office Supplies. This suggests 
that Technology products are the primary revenue drivers of the business. 

Regional Performance 

The West region contributes the highest share of total sales, while the South region shows the lowest performance. This 
indicates a regional imbalance that may require targeted strategies. 
Profitability Analysis 
Although sales are high, profit margins remain moderate at 12.33%. The analysis reveals that some transactions generate 
low or even negative profit despite high sales values. 

Discount Impact on Profit 

A negative relationship is observed between discounts and profit. Higher discounts are associated with reduced 
profitability, with some heavily discounted sales resulting in losses. Over the years profit has become less. Costs are quite 
high relative to sales. 

Shipping and Delivery Analysis 

Standard Class shipping accounts for the highest shipping costs, indicating it is the most frequently used method. Most 
deliveries are completed within 4–5 days, showing consistent logistics performance. 

Discussion 

The overall performance Indicates moderate profitability with room for margin improvement. Sales show fluctuations 
over time with an overall increasing trend toward later periods. Some periods experience sharp drops, suggesting 
seasonality or operational issues. 

As technology generates the highest sales, Product mix is heavily dependent on Technology. No extreme regional 
dependency is seen, which is good for risk diversification. The inconsistent relationship between sales and profits 
suggests that high sales do not always guarantee high profit. In fact over-discounting is reducing profitability.  
As most orders fall within 4-6 shipping days, which indicate the consistency of operations 

Recommendation 

Reduce excessive discounting, especially where it leads to losses. Invest more in Technology products (marketing, 
inventory, promotions). Improve strategy for Furniture & Office Supplies (bundling, pricing, promotions).  
Operate standard class mode continuously  to attract more customers. Review Standard Class shipping costs and explore 
cost reductions. Encourage customers toward cost-efficient shipping modes. Investigate causes of sales drops 
(seasonality, stock issues, demand changes).  

Conclusion 

The company demonstrates strong sales performance but faces challenges in profitability due to discounting and cost 
inefficiencies. By optimizing pricing strategies, focusing on high-performing categories, and improving operational 
efficiency, overall business performance can be significantly enhanced.
